\begin{frame}{Principle Tensions: The Art of Trade-offs}
\note{
% -- LINK: What prior concept connects to this slide
The six principles must all be satisfied. But they sometimes CONFLICT ---
this slide reveals the tensions engineers must navigate.

% -- NARRATE: What to SAY while showing this slide
[3 min] Walk through the diagram: ``Communication optimization increases
synchronization, which increases failure exposure. Sustainability limits
raw infrastructure choices. Responsible AI adds 10--20 ms latency to serving.''
Key insight: ``The art of distributed ML engineering is finding designs that
balance all six within acceptable trade-offs.''

% -- WARN: What students will get wrong on THIS topic
Common error: students optimize one principle and ignore the others. Correct
framing: production systems must satisfy all six simultaneously --- a system
that is fast but unfair, or robust but unsustainable, fails to deploy.

\section{The Complete System}

\begin{frame}{The Complete Production System}
\note{
% -- LINK: What prior concept connects to this slide
Students mastered competencies in isolation. This slide integrates them into
the complete production system --- showing how infrastructure, training,
operations, and governance connect as an interdependent whole.

% -- NARRATE: What to SAY while showing this slide
[3 min] Walk through the four layers bottom to top: ``Infrastructure: compute,
network, storage --- the physical foundation. Training: distributed optimization,
fault tolerance --- building the model. Operations: serving, monitoring, CI/CD ---
running the model. Governance: security, sustainability, responsibility --- the
constraints that determine whether the system should run at all.'' Key insight:
``A failure at any layer propagates to all layers above. A GPU fault corrupts
training. A training failure produces a bad model. A bad model violates
governance constraints. The system is only as strong as its weakest layer.''

% -- ENGAGE: Specific question, prediction, or task for THIS slide
Ask: ``Which layer failure is hardest to detect?'' Give 10 seconds.
[Expected: governance --- fairness degradation and sustainability overruns
are the most insidious because they accumulate slowly without triggering
infrastructure alarms.]

% -- WARN: What students will get wrong on THIS topic
Common error: students think the layers are independent. Correct framing:
the layers are tightly coupled --- a 15\% security tax at the infrastructure
layer reduces training throughput, which delays operations deployment, which
affects governance SLAs.

% -- FLEX: [CORE] + contingency
[CORE] The integrated view is the chapter's unique contribution.
IF SHORT: Name the four layers and state the weakest-link principle.
}

\footnotesize
\begin{columns}[T]
  \begin{column}{0.55\textwidth}
    \textbf{Four interdependent layers:}

    \vspace{0.1cm}
    \textcolor{datastroke}{\textbf{L4: Governance}}\\
    {\scriptsize Security, sustainability, responsibility --- determines \emph{whether} to deploy}

    \vspace{0.08cm}
    \textcolor{routingstroke}{\textbf{L3: Operations}}\\
    {\scriptsize Serving, monitoring, CI/CD --- determines \emph{how well} it runs}

    \vspace{0.08cm}
    \textcolor{computestroke}{\textbf{L2: Training}}\\
    {\scriptsize Distributed optimization, fault tolerance --- determines \emph{what} the model learns}

    \vspace{0.08cm}

    GPU fault $\to$ corrupted training\\
    Bad model $\to$ SLA violation\\
    SLA violation $\to$ governance failure\\[0.1cm]

\begin{frame}{Fallacies}
\note{
% -- NARRATE: What to SAY while showing this slide
[2 min] Read each fallacy. These address the most common misconceptions
students carry out of the course. The first is critical: scale does not
just make things bigger --- it creates qualitatively new phenomena.

% -- FLEX: [CORE] + contingency
[CORE] Fallacies correct end-of-course misconceptions.
IF SHORT: Read fallacies 1 and 3 only.
}

\footnotesize
\textbf{Fallacy:} \textit{Distributed ML is just single-machine ML with more GPUs.}\\
{\scriptsize Scale creates qualitatively new phenomena: stragglers, network partitions, Byzantine faults, and heterogeneity. None exist on a single machine.}

\vspace{0.08cm}
\textbf{Fallacy:} \textit{You can optimize one principle and ignore the others.}\\
{\scriptsize A system that is fast but unfair, or robust but unsustainable, fails to deploy. All six principles must be satisfied simultaneously.}

\vspace{0.08cm}
\textbf{Fallacy:} \textit{The next breakthrough requires a bigger model.}\\
{\scriptsize The Era of Scaling is flattening. The 100$\times$ decomposition shows 10$\times$ from orchestration vs.\ 4$\times$ from hardware. Systems engineering is the highest-leverage investment.}

\vspace{0.08cm}
\textbf{Fallacy:} \textit{Governance is separate from engineering.}\\
{\scriptsize GDPR fines reach 4\% of global revenue. EU AI Act mandates energy reporting. Governance is a hard constraint, not a soft aspiration.}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Pitfalls}
\note{
% -- NARRATE: What to SAY while showing this slide
[2 min] Read each pitfall. These are operational warnings for students
entering the field.

% -- FLEX: [CORE] + contingency
[CORE] Pitfalls provide actionable warnings for new practitioners.
IF SHORT: Cover the first two pitfalls only.
}

\footnotesize
\textbf{Pitfall:} \textit{Optimizing for a single layer of the Fleet Stack.}\\
A training optimization that doubles throughput but triples network traffic reduces end-to-end efficiency. Always evaluate changes across all four layers: infrastructure, training, operations, governance.

\vspace{0.15cm}
\textbf{Pitfall:} \textit{Treating the six principles as a checklist rather than a system of tensions.}\\
Communication optimization increases synchronization, which increases failure exposure. Sustainability limits infrastructure choices. The art is navigating trade-offs, not checking boxes.

\vspace{0.15cm}
\textbf{Pitfall:} \textit{Assuming today's best practices are tomorrow's.}\\
Post-silicon technologies (optical interconnect, neuromorphic computing) will reshape the landscape. The enduring skill is quantitative reasoning about constraints --- not memorizing current solutions.

\end{frame}
