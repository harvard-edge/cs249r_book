MLSysBook Playground

Fast, playable systems simulations for ML training, serving, and infrastructure.

Browser mini-games that turn ML systems concepts — pruning, GPU memory, quantization, pipeline parallelism, batch sizing, MoE routing, serving, networking, and scheduling — into short interactive loops. Part of the MLSysBook ecosystem.

Each playground game takes about a minute to play and teaches one ML systems tradeoff through motion, pressure, and scoring. The goal is not retro arcade nostalgia; it is to make the invisible parts of training and serving feel operational.

Gradient Lander

Balance batch size and learning rate to converge safely.

Pilot your model down the loss landscape to the global minimum. “Thrust” increases your Batch Size—giving you stable descent, but rapidly burning through your limited VRAM budget. Tilt to steer your Learning Rate. Hit the minimum too fast, or run out of VRAM, and your model diverges.

Play Gradient Lander

Read: Vol II: Large Batch Training & Convergence
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Lunar Lander (1979)

Pipeline Pacer

Keep the GPUs fed without bubbling.

Schedule micro-batches across four pipeline stages. If you send too many forwards, memory fills up; if you pause too long, later stages sit idle (pipeline bubble). Balance forward and backward scheduling to maximize throughput.

Play Pipeline Pacer

Read: Vol II: Pipeline Parallelism
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Flight Control meets Overcooked

Tensor Tetris

Pack training memory before you OOM.

Parameters anchor at the bottom; activations, gradients, and optimizer states fall in their real proportions. Activations dominate — and that’s why you’ll OOM.

Play Tensor Tetris

Read: Vol I: Training Memory Constraints
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Tetris meets Dr. Mario

Pulse Prune

Shrink a network without breaking it.

A neural network on a canvas. You’ve got 45 seconds to remove most of the weights without crashing accuracy. The faint ones are safe; the bright ones are doing real work.

Play Pulse Prune

Read: Vol I: Model Compression & Pruning
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Operation meets Jenga

Quantization Sharp Shot

Compress a model before the target blurs.

Shoot a downrange target with per-layer precision dials as your sight. Lower precision saves bits but blurs, jitters, or drifts the target depending on which layer you compressed.

Play Quantization Sharp Shot

Read: Vol I: Mixed-Precision Quantization
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Silent Scope meets Duck Hunt

Batch Size Balancer

Push throughput to the edge of OOM.

A tug-of-war against time. Increase batch size to process more images per second, but watch the memory gauge—if it spikes into the red, you OOM and lose the run.

Play Batch Size Balancer

Read: Vol I: Throughput vs. Memory
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Flappy Bird (staying in the safe band)

MoE Router

Distribute tokens, balance the experts.

Tokens fly toward your Mixture of Experts layer. Swipe to route them to the specialized experts without overloading any single one. If an expert’s queue fills up, tokens drop.

Play MoE Router

Read: Vol II: Mixture of Experts
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Missile Command meets Fruit Ninja

Data Loader Dash

The CPU preparing data before the GPU starves.

Data blocks slide from left to right toward a glowing “GPU” box. Type the correct letter when the block is in the processing zone to process it. If an unprocessed block hits the GPU, the GPU starves.

Play Data Loader Dash

Read: Vol I: Data Engineering
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Overcooked meets Typing of the Dead

Checkpoint Roulette

Fault tolerance and checkpointing at scale.

Hold Spacebar to train your model. Beware of hidden “Node Failure” lightning strikes. If Spacebar is held during a strike, your progress resets. Release safely to write a checkpoint.

Play Checkpoint Roulette

Read: Vol II: Fault Tolerance
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Push-Your-Luck

Roofline Rider

The Roofline model: Compute vs Memory bound.

Navigate your kernel through operational intensity without hitting the memory or compute ceilings. Dodge incoming vertical walls while staying under the neon Roofline.

Play Roofline Rider

Read: Vol I: Hardware Acceleration
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Flappy Bird meets Race The Sun

All-Reduce Rhythm

Keep the gradients flowing in a perfect ring.

GPUs are arranged in a ring. A visual metronome expands to hit the outer wire every second. Press 1, 2, 3, or 4 exactly on the beat to fire a gradient chunk to the next GPU clockwise. Perfect timing keeps the pipeline full and increases your combo.

Play All-Reduce Rhythm

Read: Vol II: Collective Communication
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Guitar Hero meets Pipe Mania

Topology Tycoon

Build the fabric, avoid the bottlenecks.

You have 8 GPUs and 20 seconds to build their network topology. Click between two nodes to toggle their connection: None, InfiniBand, or NVLink. Once the run phase begins, packets will flood the network. Maximize your delivered GB/s!

Play Topology Tycoon

Read: Vol II: Network Fabrics
🕹️ Arcade DNA: SimCity meets Mini Motorways

KV Cache Packer

Fit the prompts before memory fragments.

A Tetris-like grid of requests. Place contiguous blocks to serve them. As they finish at different times, your memory fragments. Unlock Paged Mode to shatter requests into 1x1 blocks and defrag your cache!

Play KV Cache Packer

Read: Vol II: LLM Serving
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Tetris meets Sandfall

Cluster Commander

Schedule jobs without fragmenting the fleet.

Schedule 1x1, 2x2, and 4x4 jobs on an 8x8 GPU cluster. Don’t let scattered small jobs fragment the cluster, or a massive 4x4 pre-training run will block the entire queue and kill your utilization!

Play Cluster Commander

Read: Vol II: Fleet Orchestration
🕹️ Arcade DNA: Block Puzzle

MLSysBook Playground is part of mlsysbook.ai. The games share design vocabulary with the textbook — compute blue, data green, error red, MIT accent — so a figure in the book and a game in your browser feel like the same world.
